2. Ministry Strategy: The National Infrastructure Pitch
For ministries you are not selling a product. You are selling a solution to unemployment friction. The language changes completely.

MIFOTRA: Ministry of Public Service and Labour
Angle: Digital Meritocracy.
Pitch Brydg as the tool to handle civil service exams and initial interviews. Every candidate is interviewed and scored by the same neutral AI agent. No human bias. No nepotism. A documented, auditable process that the ministry can stand behind publicly.

MINICT: Ministry of ICT and Innovation
Angle: Tech-Enabled Workforce.
Request integration into the Irembo portal or recognition as a standard tool for the Digital Ambassadors programme. Brydg becomes part of Rwanda's national digital infrastructure narrative.

National Employment Programme: Kora Wigire
Angle: Mass Screening at Scale.
They move thousands of people through TVET vocational training. Brydg acts as the exit interview to certify graduates are job-ready. This is a government endorsement that unlocks every other conversation in Rwanda.

3. Direct Company Outreach: Same Price, Same Model
Every company that hires is a potential client. Recruitment agencies are the first channel but they are not the only one. Banks, telcos, NGOs, hospitality groups and parastatals all run hiring cycles and all have the same pain.
The pricing is identical: $29 per month plus $1 per completed interview. No separate enterprise pricing. No confusion. The simplicity is the strategy.

Why Same Pricing Works for Direct Companies
  A bank interviewing 200 candidates pays $229 total for that month.
  That is cheaper than the electricity and coffee bill for 200 human-led interviews.
  There is no negotiation. The math does the selling.
